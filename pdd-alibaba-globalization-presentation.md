# Presentation: PDD Holdings & Alibaba Group in a Globalized Market

**Globalization & Platform Power: PDD + Alibaba**

## Slide 2 — Who We Are (Context)
- **Group identity:** PDD Holdings + Alibaba Group Holdings Limited
- **Industry role:** Two of the world’s largest integrated digital trade ecosystems
- **Core capabilities:**
  - E-commerce marketplaces
  - Logistics coordination and fulfillment
  - Data-driven retail optimization

**Evidence:** Temu (PDD) has expanded into **nearly 100 markets**, while Alibaba operates major domestic and international commerce platforms.

---

## Slide 3 — What We Stand For (Priorities)
### Strategic Priorities
1. **Scale global influence** while maintaining operational efficiency
2. **Compete with Western giants (especially Amazon)**
3. **Increase PTP (price-to-performance) value** for consumers
4. Deliver **low-cost, high-volume products** with reliable delivery

### Operating Priorities
- Merchant growth + product assortment
- Supply chain speed and resilience
- Consumer trust and compliance credibility

---

## Slide 4 — Globalization Force #1: Cross-Border Trade Rules
- Growth is no longer just “lowest price wins”
- Success now depends on handling:
  - Tariffs
  - Customs controls
  - Product-safety and import documentation rules

**Specific evidence:**
- The **EU is moving toward stricter duties/compliance for low-value parcels** (including tighter oversight of high-volume cross-border shipments).

**Implication for our group:**
- Compliance infrastructure becomes a competitive advantage, not just a legal requirement.

---

## Slide 5 — Globalization Force #2: Platform Regulation
- Governments are increasing scrutiny of platform behavior
- New expectations include:
  - Pricing transparency
  - Fair treatment of merchants
  - Clearer accountability for platform practices

**Specific evidence:**
- **China has proposed stronger platform-governance rules** emphasizing pricing transparency and merchant fairness.

**Implication for our group:**
- Regulatory trust directly affects long-term market access and brand legitimacy.

---

## Slide 6 — Globalization Force #3: International Competition
- Competitive pressure is increasing in North America, Europe, and emerging markets
- Key rivals: **Amazon** and regional e-commerce platforms
- Differentiator for PDD + Alibaba:
  - Cost efficiency at scale
  - Fast SKU rotation
  - Data-informed demand matching

**Risk:** Geopolitical tensions can quickly reshape market entry costs and policy barriers.

---

## Slide 7 — Our Position (Clear POV)
**Main claim:** Globalization now rewards firms that combine **price advantage + compliance capability + platform accountability**.

So, our strategy is to:
- Keep value pricing and product breadth
- Invest in compliance and safety systems
- Strengthen logistics/localization per region
- Compete on trust and execution, not price alone
